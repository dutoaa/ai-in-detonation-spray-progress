Naman Jain, Mohit Bansal, Abhishek Pandey, Atul Garg, Prem Narayan Vishwakarma, Siddharth Jain

Friction stir welding (FSW) is a solid-state joining process characterized by a non-linear and complex relationship between the process parameters and joint strength. In the present work, the prediction of FSW joint strength is done through a comprehensive data-driven framework along with parameter sensitivity analysis. Different machine learning (ML) models have been applied along with hyper-tuning parameter optimization to predict the FSW joint strength. The data-driven framework consists of 275 experimental datasets systematically collected from the peer-reviewed literature. The effect of FSW parameters (such as joint type, plate thickness, pin height, tool tilt angle, and tool shoulder to pin diameter ratio) on the joint strength has been examined. Results revealed that the highest accuracy was yielded by the Random Forest Regression and Artificial Neural Network models, followed by the K -Nearest Neighbors and Support Vector Regression; thereby highlighting the efficacy of ML in modeling multifactorial welding processes. Furthermore, SHapley Additive exPlanation analysis has been performed to obtain the feature importance and their impact on joint strength prediction. The tool rotational speed and plate thickness were the most influential parameters affecting the joint strength, as per the analysis. Also, the contribution of tool traverse speed and pin height was moderate but process-dependent. This integrated approach not only enhances the interpretability of ML models but also provides actionable insights for process optimization; particularly in dissimilar welding scenarios where parameter sensitivity is delicate due to material incompatibilities. It was also found that the tool rotational speed and plate thickness were the dominant variables on which the joint strength depends. Process-dependent effects exhibited by the tool traverse speed and pin height were linked to heat input and material flow. Dissimilar joints displayed higher sensitivity to parameter variations as compared to the similar joints. Sen Li, Xiaoying Liu, Xiaojian Xu, Chendong Shao, Yaqi Wang, Ling Lan

The laser welding full-penetration is of critical importance, as it constitutes one of the fundamental factors in achieving defect-free welded joints. Accurate prediction of the penetration state is therefore essential for ensuring weld quality. To this end, this paper introduces SimPhysNet, a novel algorithm that achieves high classification accuracy in laser welding penetration prediction using only a limited number of labelled images. This approach effectively overcomes the limitations of supervised learning classification algorithms, which are hindered in industrial applications by their dependence on extensive, high-quality labelled data. The core of SimPhysNet is a unique self-supervised learning paradigm that embeds physical priors into a contrastive learning framework. By incorporating a physics-informed neural network (PINN), the model is guided to extract physically meaningful features of the molten pool and keyhole from a large set of unlabelled data, while three image augmentation tasks further enhance its generalization capabilities. Subsequently, a few-shot learning strategy, based on prototypical networks, enables robust classification by constructing class representations from a minimal set of labelled images. Experimental results demonstrate that SimPhysNet achieves a classification accuracy of 96.06% using only 200 labelled images (approximately 5% of the total labelled dataset), which is comparable to the performance of conventional supervised learning algorithms that utilize the entire labelled dataset. Computational and machine learning modelling approaches for weld quality predictions in friction stir welding of high-density polyethylene用于高密度聚乙烯搅拌摩擦焊焊缝质量预测的计算与机器学习建模方法

A. Alhourani, I. Barsoum, J. Sheikh-Ahmad, S. Deveci

Friction stir welding (FSW) has gained recognition as an effective technique for joining thermoplastic materials. This study presents a comprehensive investigation to predict FSW characteristics such as weld temperatures, vertical axial forces, material flow and defect formation in FSW of high-density polyethylene (HDPE) using both computational and machine learning approaches, validated by experimental results. A three-dimensional Coupled Eulerian-Lagrangian (CEL) numerical model is developed to simulate the HDPE welding process under varying process parameters of rotational and welding speeds, and bottom preheating conditions. The CEL numerical model is validated against experimental data, showing markedly good agreement in predicting void formation, thermal and mechanical responses. Results indicate that preheating the bottom surface proved effective in reducing thermal gradients and stabilizing vertical force fluctuations. Quantitatively, bottom-surface preheating at 60 °C reduced peak plunge force from 1600 N close to 1100 N and raised peak temperatures from 70 °C (without preheat) to about 100 °C, hence smoothing through-thickness gradients and stabilizing the axial force distribution.
